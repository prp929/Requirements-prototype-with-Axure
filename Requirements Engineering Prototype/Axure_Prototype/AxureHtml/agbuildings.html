Barns/Polebarns

Barns - Barns are traditional agricultural structures used to house animals and store feed and equipment. They can be made of wood, metal, or a combination of materials, and come in many different shapes and sizes. Barns typically have a large open space with individual stalls or pens for animals on one side, and a storage area for feed and equipment on the other side.

Greenhouses

Greenhouses are agricultural structures used to grow plants in a controlled environment. They are typically made of glass or plastic and allow sunlight in while trapping heat inside. Greenhouses can be used to grow a variety of crops, including vegetables, fruits, and flowers.

Silos and Grain Bins

 Silos are tall cylindrical structures used to store grain, hay, or silage. They can be made of concrete, metal, or other materials. Silos protect the stored crops from moisture, pests, and other environmental factors that could damage or spoil them. They also help farmers to store crops for longer periods of time.

Chicken Coops- Large or Small

Chicken coops are small agricultural structures used to house chickens. They are typically made of wood and wire mesh, and are designed to provide a safe and comfortable environment for the birds. Chicken coops can be used to raise chickens for eggs or meat, and come in a variety of shapes and sizes to accommodate different flock sizes.
