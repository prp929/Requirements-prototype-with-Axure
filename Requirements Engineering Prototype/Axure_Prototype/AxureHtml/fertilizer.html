Herbicides


Herbicides are chemicals used to kill or control weeds. They are typically sprayed onto crops or soil and work by disrupting the growth or metabolism of the weeds. Some herbicides are selective and only target certain types of weeds, while others are non-selective and can damage or kill any plant they come into contact with.

Insecticides

Insecticides are chemicals used to kill or control insects. They can be applied to crops, soil, or other surfaces where insects may be present. Insecticides work by disrupting the nervous system or other physiological processes of the insects, and can be selective or non-selective depending on the specific product.

Fungicides

Fungicides are chemicals used to control or prevent fungal diseases in crops. They can be applied to plants, soil, or other surfaces to prevent the growth or spread of fungal pathogens. Fungicides work by disrupting the metabolism or cell walls of the fungi, and can be either systemic (absorbed by the plant) or non-systemic (applied directly to the affected area).

Fertilizers

Fertilizers are chemicals used to provide essential nutrients to crops. They can be synthetic or organic, and are typically applied to soil or directly to plants. Fertilizers contain nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, and other essential nutrients that are necessary for plant growth and development. Different types of fertilizers may be used depending on the specific crop and soil conditions.
